Jhumka

The word 'jhumki' refers to the bell-shaped earrings
which are available in various designs and sizes and can be
worn on any occasion or routinely. Jhumkas represent the
timeless beauty in jewellery that emerged in olden days and
has managed to retain its popularity across cultures. Polki
jhumkas have tremendous versatility. They can be worn daily
or on special occasions such as weddings and parties.

Kundan Necklace

The term ‘kundan’ itself means highly refined
pure gold, and this type of jewellery generally
involves 24k pure gold. Kundan jewellery holds
credit as one of the oldest forms of
jewellery in India with a rich legacy of over 2,500 years.

Nath

This fine gold nose ring or nath is decorated with
multiple pearls and rubies. Mostly worn by Hindu
women in India, such nose rings or naths also were
worn by Indian Muslim women who, historically,
have been acculturated with Hindu ways.

Tikli

The tikli is a mainstay in bridal jewellery. A pendant
is suspended from a chain and is worn in the hair
the parting. Its design usually matches the main
wedding set. Even as early as a decade ago, the
tikli would gather dust in the deep, dark recesses
of a woman's jewellery vault but not anymore.
It has become an essential piece for not just
weddings but a tikli can now be worn for any
traditional event
